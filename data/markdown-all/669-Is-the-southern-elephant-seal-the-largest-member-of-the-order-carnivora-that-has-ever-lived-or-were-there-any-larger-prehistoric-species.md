## Is the southern elephant seal the largest member of the order carnivora that has ever lived or were there any larger prehistoric species?
    
**Upvotes**: 7 | **Comments**: 0 | **Date**: [3y](https://www.quora.com/Is-the-southern-elephant-seal-the-largest-member-of-the-order-carnivora-that-has-ever-lived-or-were-there-any-larger-prehistoric-species/answer/Gary-Meaney)

The largest extinct carnivoran currently known is Pontolis magnus, a stem-odobenid pinniped, which is thought to have weighed in at about 4 tonnes. Southern elephant seals weigh 5.5 tonnes, so to answer your question - yes, the southern elephant seal is the largest member of the order Carnivora.
